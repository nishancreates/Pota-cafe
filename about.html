How It All Began

Born from a Dream
and a Good Bean

Pota had a simple wish: she wanted a cafe that felt the way Boudha felt — spiritual, warm, unhurried. A place where the first sip of coffee would make you exhale and settle into yourself.

She found Chef Kamusari at a small roastery in Patan. He was already doing extraordinary things with Nepali highland beans and a single espresso machine. Together, they built Pota Cafe from scratch — two chairs, one espresso machine, and an unwavering belief that good coffee changes days.

Four years later, our regulars are the heartbeat of this place. Their names are written on cups. Their usual orders are memorised. That's the cafe we set out to build — and that's the cafe we're proud to be.

What Drives Us

The Heart of
Everything We Do

Bean Obsession

We source directly from Nepali highland farmers and trusted single-origin roasters. Every bean is cupped before it reaches your cup. No shortcuts.

Made by Hand

Every pastry baked that morning. Every coffee pulled by a trained barista. No batch brewing. No frozen dough. Everything is made with intention.

The People Behind the Cup

Meet the Team

Founder & Owner

Pota

A Kathmandu native with a decade spent travelling and tasting cafes across Asia. She brought the best of what she found home — and built the cafe Boudha deserved. She knows every regular by name and every bean by origin.

Head Chef & Head Barista

Chef Kamusari

Trained as a barista in Melbourne and a pastry chef in Osaka. Kamusari returned to Nepal with one mission: to prove that Nepali coffee deserves world-class treatment. His espresso technique is the stuff of local legend.

Our Journey

Four Years of
Beautiful Mornings

From two chairs and a dream to the most loved cafe in Boudha — every year brought new beans, new faces, and new reasons to keep brewing.

2019

The Idea Brews

Pota returns from a year-long cafe crawl across Thailand, Japan and South Korea with a notebook full of ideas and a deep love for slow coffee culture.

2020

Meeting Kamusari

A chance encounter at a Patan roastery. Kamusari is pulling single-origin shots. Pota tastes the best espresso of her life. They shake hands and start planning.

2021

Pota Cafe Opens

Opening day: 6 tables, 2 staff, 1 espresso machine. Within the first week, every table is full by 9am. Boudha had been waiting for this.

2022

Kitchen Expands

Kamusari launches the full brunch menu. The Mushroom Avocado Toast immediately goes viral on Instagram. Queue forms before opening.

2025

Still Here, Still Brewing

Over 8,000 guests. A menu that changes with the seasons. The same espresso machine, now joined by three more. Same love. Every single day.
